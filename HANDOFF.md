## What it is
A grimy dark-fantasy **idle auto-battler**. Build a horde of rats; it auto-rides a gauntlet every hour and hauls back scrap by how deep it pushed. Spend scrap to grow the horde, or leave it running. Wrapped in a **synchronized 7-day expedition** that resets weekly (Monday 06:00 CET); a global leaderboard ranks by **deepest wave reached that week**.

## Live game ‚Äî the core loop
- Horde rides **hourly** ‚Üí **+1 scrap per depth** cleared (`SCRAP_PER_DEPTH = 1`). Income accrues live + offline (‚â§24h per visit, `OFFLINE_RIDE_CAP`). Depth/income update live as you build.
- **Hourly variance (shuffle-only):** each ride reshuffles wave composition under the fixed **daily theme** (`generateGauntlet(date, day, hour)`; hourless calls stay byte-identical for golden logs). Same budgets/archetype quotas, ~¬±1‚Äì2 wave swing; the scout report stays truthful all day.
- **Ride log** (last 24 rides: time ¬∑ depth ¬∑ **N felled** ¬∑ scrap, ‚òÖ on the season-best row, ‚öë badge on a full clear). Shown in the idle panel.
- **Season best = completed rides only** (the "watch the next ride" preview does not count). The best ride's hour is stored and sent as `rideHour` inside the lineup jsonb for future anti-cheat re-sim.
- **"Rats felled this week"** (`seasonKills`): cumulative enemies defeated across completed rides, climbs all week, resets Monday. Shown in the idle panel + per ride-log row + leaderboard rows. It's the **leaderboard tiebreak** behind depth.
- **7-day expedition:** board cap grows **5‚Üí8** across the week, scrap carries across days, full reset after day 7 / at the Monday season boundary.
- **Replay** ("‚ñ∂ watch the next ride"): a live preview of the current build. **All players** now get **1√ó/2√ó/4√ó speed** controls + a **"‚è≠ to final wave"** button (fast-forwards to the last wave, then plays it at normal speed) ‚Äî previously dev-only.
- **3 promo infographics** at `‚Ä¶/we-ride-at-dawn/promo/` (gameplay, minions/relics, economy) + gallery.

## ‚öÝ Live exploit + the compounding law (2026-07-08 ‚Äî read this before touching abilities)

**The 45-wave loop makes any repeatable permanent effect unbounded.** `simulate` runs one persistent horde through 45 waves. Two exploits came from ignoring that; assume a third is waiting.

1. **`startOfBattle` fired every wave** (`fireStartOfBattle` was *inside* the wave loop). Enemies re-instantiate per wave so they were fine; the horde is not. Four tier-3 Warren-Wardens re-buffing `+1/+1 to all behind` each wave took a 6-attack rat to **241**, and depth-in-Wardens *accelerated* (marginal +3.5, +4.1, +4.4, +5.2). The live #1 board rode it to **44/45 on day 3**. Fixed on `dev`: `startOfBattle` now fires **once per unit instance, ever**; a new **`startOfWave`** trigger fires every wave and is reserved for **non-accumulating** effects (summon a body that dies, poison that clears at `waveClear`). Rat-Piper + Plague-Bearer moved to `startOfWave`. Board 44 ‚Üí **15**.
2. **Two Bone-Priests revived each other forever** ‚Äî 12 scrap, tier 1, no relics, **45/45 full clear**, ~12k revives. The 0.6.2 fix only stopped *self*-revive; a revived priest that dies re-enters `fallen`. Fixed by a **`raised` flag** (a corpse may be raised once per battle). 45 ‚Üí 1. **This is live on prod right now** (`69f47ba` on `dev` is the standalone cherry-pick).

**The law:** before adding any trigger/effect, ask *"what bounds this across 45 waves?"* ‚Äî and write the answer in the code comment.
- üî¥ **`gainStats` on `afterAttack` must never ship unclamped.** `afterAttack` fires **per tick** (`MAX_TICKS_PER_WAVE = 1000`) ‚Äî two orders of magnitude faster than the Warden bug. This kills ROADMAP's proposed **Gutter-Butcher** (`afterAttack: gainStats +1/+0`) and the **Blood-Debt** relic ("+1 attack permanently *this battle*" ‚Äî "this battle" is 45 waves) as written.
- ‚öÝÔ∏è **`gainStats` on `allyFaint`** (Corpse-Glutton) is still linear-in-waves: a t3 Piper re-feeds a t3 Glutton `+9/+9` per wave. Plateaus ~12‚Äì15 in practice, left alone deliberately ‚Äî capping at +12 overshoots downward. If it ever needs a clamp, `gainStatsCap = 24`.
- ‚öÝÔ∏è **`tailCharmUsed` is never reset** ‚Äî Tail-Charm is once per *45-wave battle* while Glass Shard was deliberately made once per *wave*. Inconsistent; it's dead last on Œî/scrap (0.021). Probably wants the per-wave treatment (that's a buff ‚Üí Monday change).
- Checked clean: poison (both kinds), `faint` buffs/summons, `onFaintDamageAll`, `healPerTick`, the `resolveDeaths` loop.

**Post-fix balance:** Warren-Warden is still #1 but no longer a different species (Œî/scrap `0.181 ‚Üí 0.107` vs Corpse-Glutton's unchanged `0.101`; lead 1.79√ó ‚Üí 1.06√ó). Value/scrap spread narrows **21.8√ó ‚Üí 12.9√ó** (real progress on the ROADMAP ¬ß4 cost‚Üîvalue item). **Gore-Cleaver overtakes Fat Tick as the top relic** (0.121 vs 0.115) ‚Äî attack throughput finally beats sustain, which was the whole point of the 0.6.0 rework. A strong day-7 board now peaks ~**14.5**; target was 15‚Äì20. The knob to close it is **softening** the enemy curve, not steepening it: `ENEMY_HEALTH_SCALE_PER_WAVE/QUADRATIC/ATTACK 0.30/0.008/0.07` ‚Üí d7 **15.6** (day-1 depth barely moves, 3.52 ‚Üí 3.57); `0.25/0.006/0.06` ‚Üí **16.8**. **Don't touch `WAVE_COUNT`** ‚Äî it's a ceiling, not a difficulty knob. **‚öÝ `npm run snowball` and `npm run balance:depth` both report pre-fix inflated numbers ‚Äî re-run them before trusting any tuning.**

## Combat & balance model (0.6.0 ‚Äî the big rework this release)
- **Front-clash sim:** the frontmost horde unit trades simultaneous damage with the frontmost enemy; **overkill past a kill is discarded** (unless a cleave relic carries it). No back-row targeting; AoE only via specific relics/abilities.
- **Depth ceiling raised 12 ‚Üí 45** (`WAVE_COUNT`). It's an aspirational horizon ‚Äî strong play reaches ~10‚Äì16, nobody's near 45.
- **Difficulty scales by WAVE DEPTH, not by day.** Enemy stats scale on wave index `w` at instantiation in `sim.ts`: health `√ó(1 + 0.35¬∑w + 0.012¬∑w¬≤)`, attack `√ó(1 + 0.08¬∑w)` ‚Äî so deep foes are **HP sponges** and attack finally matters. **Day-scaling was removed** (`difficultyForDay` returns **1**); the wave-budget quadratic was cut `0.15 ‚Üí 0.05`. Per-day achievable depth is **monotonic ~4 (day 1) ‚Üí ~12 (day 7), peaking day 7** ‚Äî you go deeper because your roster grew, not because early days were easier. This was a deliberate fix so there's **no "peak mid-week and coast"** incentive (the leaderboard metric is max depth over the whole week).
- **Gore-Cleaver relic** (unit, cost 5, `cleaveOverkill`): a killing blow carries its overkill to the next foe (single target, no chaining). It's the lever that makes attack scale against the HP-sponge curve ‚Äî but it's **back-loaded** (weak early, ~17√ó Rusted Nail's depth delta but still modest in absolute terms, ~+0.25 waves at day 7). Making attack *punchier* (an execute / on-kill relic) is a roadmap item.
- **New tanky enemy:** Sluice-Bulwark (atk 2 / hp 16, armored). No SVG art yet (falls back to a rect in replay).
- **Relics ‚Äî one of each per carrier:** a rat carries each trinket once, the horde each team relic once; merges pool trinkets but dedupe. Kills degenerate Rusted-Nail stacking and no-op duplicate Tail-Charm while keeping combos. A relic lost to the merge-dedup is **refunded at 50%** (`floor(cost/2)`, min 1 ‚Äî the unit sell rate; a full refund let you buy power for free early), added 0.6.1.
- **Poison note:** poison is flat-per-tick and depth-independent, so it scales *relatively* better as foes get tankier ‚Äî a one-off sim showed it did **not** dominate attack builds, but it hasn't had a broad roster sweep (watch it).
- **Bone-Priest revive fix (0.6.2 hotfix):** `revive` now raises the oldest fallen *ally*, never the caster. Closes an exploit where a lone tier-1 Bone-Priest revived *itself* forever (fainting queues you into `fallen` before your own faint fires) and solo-cleared all 45 waves for 6 scrap. Found by the snowball harness; was live on prod but the board wasn't exploited; hotfixed same day as 0.6.2. Regression test in `abilities.test.ts`.
- **Glass Shard reworked (on `dev`, ships in 0.6.3):** its +3 first-hit now fires **once per wave** instead of once per battle (the horde's first-hit flag resets at each wave start in `sim.ts`). Sim: dead weight (+0.008 depth) ‚Üí **+0.192 (0.048/scrap)**, on par with Rusted Nail, below Fat Tick.

## Economy & shop
- **Costs are ~2√ó a baseline; starting scrap 24** (`DAILY_SCRAP`). Cheapest units cost 2‚Äì6, so a fresh player can recruit ~4‚Äì6 rats immediately (a first-run hint now says so).
- **Interest:** paid **daily at dawn**, 5% of bank, floored, **capped at +5** (`INTEREST_RATE 0.05`, `INTEREST_CAP 5`). **‚öÝ Likely near-vestigial now** that depth 10+ (‚âà10+ scrap/hour) is easy ‚Äî flagged to re-examine next sim run (see `wrad-interest-tuning` memory + ROADMAP economy notes).
- **Shop:** 4 unit slots + 2 relic slots (`rollOfferings`, deterministic per date/roll). **Reroll costs 1 scrap**; **freeze** holds a stall **through a reroll only** (it resets every dawn ‚Äî the copy now honestly says "keeps a stall when you reroll", not "for later").
- **Owned team relics are excluded from the shop** (0.6.0 fix): a team relic can only be held once, so `rollOfferings` filters owned ones and buying one clears any sibling stall ‚Äî no more dead unbuyable Filth-Totem slot. Unit relics still reappear (a second copy can go on another rat).
- **Board cap** grows 5‚Üí8 across the week (`BOARD_CAP = 8` hard max, incl. summons). **Summons stop when the board is full** ‚Äî so a second summoner (e.g. two Rat-Pipers) can be starved once the board caps out; there's now a clarity hint on summoner cards ("summons pause when your warren is full (8)"). A proper summon-build rework is a roadmap item.
- **The bench (0.6.1):** `BuildState.bench` (`BENCH_SIZE = 3`) stores rats *outside* the fighting horde ‚Äî hold copies while fishing for the 3rd to merge, and keep counter-units to swap against the daily theme. **Bench units never fight** (excluded from `simulate`, so no golden/sim impact). Merges resolve **across board+bench** (result lands on the board if any of the three was fighting, else on the bench). Buying past a full board **overflows to the bench**. Direct **bench‚Üîboard swap** (`swapWithBench`) from a rat's card when the board is full (no sell needed). Carries across days; old saves default `bench: []`.
